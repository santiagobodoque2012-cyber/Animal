Mammoths were important because they helped shape Ice Age grasslands and were a valuable resource for early humans. People used mammoths for food, clothing, tools, and even shelter. Today, scientists study mammoth fossils and DNA to learn more about the Ice Age, extinct animals, and how Earth's environment has changed over time.
